Web Configurator

Self-Service Installation Packages

August 2018

Product Overview

Intel offers an extensive suite of tools that support embedded systems development but downloading the entire suite of the cross-platform host and target combinations requires significant time, space, and bandwidth. The package configurator is a web-based solution for creating dynamic installation packages that support embedded developers and OEMs during the power-on and system bring-up phases of embedded development. Users choose their desired tools and receive a custom binary installer package.

Role

Responsible for UX, Interaction and visual design as well as serving on the scrum team for the first year of quarterly releases. I covered the UX aspects of taking the product from concept to launch. After our initial round of user research, I facilitated a design sprint to establish the first set of wireframes and strived to strike the right balance between technical constraints, customer/user expectations and an interface that eliminated complexity. Post launch I was responsible for product evaluation, customer feedback collection, and redesign while supporting application engineering with design assets and UX support.

Process

  • Contextual walkthrough to learn how sysadmins, developers and devops engineers acquired their software from vendors and then distributed internally.
  • Deep product discovery of various software distribution methods including binary installers, package managers and containers.
  • Collaboration and strategic planning with product managers to devise a incremental feature launch roadmap.
  • Execution of fully-interactive product prototypes for user testing and executive feedback.
