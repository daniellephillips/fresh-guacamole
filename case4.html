RST Dashboard

UX Research and Formative Design for RAID Dashboard

2015

Product Overview

The RST dashboard is a monitoring and configuration tool for systems enabled with RST (RAID) arrays and storage technology. The RST controller gathers, sends, and aggregates information about the status and performance of monitored RAID volumes. The dashboard strategy was to identify and surface the most crucial data with an easy way to navigate to areas requiring attention.

Role

Owned UX research and testing processes start to finish and led an iterative "test and fix" approach as our strategy. We first baselined usability with a fast and cheap kick-off test then aligned our test cadence to coincide a week before code freeze, leaving a week for small fixes.

Key Activities and Tools

  • Writing research plans, test protocols and recruitement screeners.
  • Moderating user study sessions.
  • Synthesizing research data to create usability reports, user stories and videos.
  • Working with front-end developers to address interaction, workflow and visual design issues.

Small Iterative Testing Rounds

Cycles of code, test, fix

Each round of research was quick and efficient with no extensive reporting because product decisions were made with the team right after the sessions. I ran quick and efficient 30 minute sessions which resulted in quick fixes through classified issues. Research was carried out on interactive prototypes and then again with the coded UI after intergration. We left buffer time before releases for minor bug fixing.
